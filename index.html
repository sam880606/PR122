Applications of Pigment Red 122 - FAQs

1. What are the main uses of Pigment Red 122?

Pigment Red 122 is a vibrant blue-shade red pigment widely used in:

  • Artist paints (oil, acrylic, watercolor, colored pencils, etc.)
  • Plastics (such as PP, PE, PVC, etc.)
  • Coatings (automotive paints, high-end industrial coatings, etc.)
  • Printing inks (offset, inkjet, UV inks, etc.)

2. Can Pigment Red 122 be added in small amounts?

Yes. PR122 has high tinting strength, and even small additions can affect the final hue, so it is often used to blend various red, purple, or pink colors.

3. How does Pigment Red 122 perform at low concentrations?

At low concentrations, PR122 still has good color saturation and does not easily turn white or darken. Its high transparency allows it to exhibit excellent layering in water-based color applications (such as watercolor or transparent inks).

4. What is the price range of Pigment Red 122?

PR122 is a cost-effective choice in applications that require vividness and durability. Compared to other high lightfastness organic pigments (such as some alizarin or quinacridone pigments), PR122 offers excellent light and weather resistance while maintaining reasonable costs.

5. Why is Pigment Red 122 called Quinacridone Red?

The chemical structure of PR122 belongs to the Quinacridone class of compounds. Its core molecular structure contains a conjugated double bond system, which gives it high stability, high lightfastness, and high transparency, so PR122 is named Quinacridone Red.

6. How safe is Pigment Red 122?

PR122 almost meets the requirements of most regulations for pigments, usually does not contain heavy metals, and complies with EU REACH regulations, US ASTM D-4236 (non-toxic certification), etc. For more detailed regulatory compliance information, please contact us to obtain real-time declarations or test reports.
